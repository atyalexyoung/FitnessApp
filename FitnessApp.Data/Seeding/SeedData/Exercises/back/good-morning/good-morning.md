## Form Cues
1. Barbell rests on upper traps, feet hip-width apart.
2. Hinge at hips, back straight, knees slightly bent.
3. Lower torso until parallel or hamstring stretch felt.
4. Return to upright position by driving hips forward.

## Common Mistakes
- Rounding spine  
- Locking knees  
- Going too deep initially  

## Variations
**Easier:**  
- Lighter barbell  
- Use safety bar  

**Harder:**  
- Pause at bottom  
- Slow eccentric  

## Programming Options
- 3–4 sets × 8–12 reps  
- Accessory for deadlift or squat day  

## Benefits
- Strengthens hamstrings, glutes, lower back  
- Improves hip hinge mechanics  
- Increases posterior chain durability  

## Tips for Success
- Keep spine neutral  
- Engage core and lats  
- Controlled descent
