## Form Cues
1. Lie prone on reverse hyper machine, hips over pad.
2. Grasp handles, legs extended behind.
3. Lift legs to hip height using glutes and lower back.
4. Lower under control, maintain neutral spine.

## Common Mistakes
- Swinging legs  
- Hyperextending lower back  
- Using momentum  

## Variations
**Easier:**  
- Lighter weight  
- Shorter range  

**Harder:**  
- Pause at top  
- Add ankle weights  

## Programming Options
- 3–4 sets × 10–15 reps  
- Good for posterior chain rehab and strength  

## Benefits
- Strengthens lower back safely  
- Builds glutes and hamstrings  
- Improves hip extension mechanics  

## Tips for Success
- Control every rep  
- Keep spine neutral  
- Avoid momentum
