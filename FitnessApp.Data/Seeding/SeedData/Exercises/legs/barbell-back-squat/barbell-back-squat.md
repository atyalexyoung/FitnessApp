## Form Cues
1. Place barbell on upper back, feet shoulder-width.
2. Descend by pushing hips back, knees tracking over toes.
3. Lower until thighs parallel or below.
4. Drive through heels to stand.

## Common Mistakes
- Knee valgus (inward collapse)  
- Heels rising  
- Rounding back  

## Variations
**Easier:**  
- Goblet squat  
- Lighter barbell  

**Harder:**  
- Pause at bottom  
- Slow eccentric  
- Heavier load  

## Programming Options
- 3–5 sets × 5–12 reps  

## Benefits
- Builds quads, glutes, hamstrings  
- Core and lower back stability  
- Foundation for strength and hypertrophy  

## Tips for Success
- Keep chest up  
- Heels on floor  
- Controlled depth
