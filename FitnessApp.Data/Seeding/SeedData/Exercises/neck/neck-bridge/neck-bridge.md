## Form Cues
1. Lie on back or stand with support.  
2. Place hands and head on the floor or mat.  
3. Lift hips, creating a bridge with head and neck supporting weight.  

## Common Mistakes
- Collapsing neck  
- Overarching lower back  
- Jerky motion  

## Variations
**Easier:**  
- Use support under head  
- Keep hips lower  

**Harder:**  
- Full bridge with legs extended  
- Hold longer  

## Programming Options
- 2–3 sets × 5–10 reps  
- Include in advanced neck strength or wrestling/football prep  

## Benefits
- Strengthens posterior neck and upper traps  
- Improves cervical and thoracic mobility  
- Enhances stability and resilience  

## Tips for Success
- Avoid pain in cervical spine  
- Engage core and glutes  
- Progress slowly
