## Form Cues
1. Sit on floor with front leg at 90°, back leg at 90°.  
2. Rotate hips to switch legs, maintaining torso upright.  
3. Keep core tight and controlled.  

## Common Mistakes
- Leaning torso  
- Losing control during switch  
- Rounding lower back  

## Variations
**Easier:**  
- Use hands for support  
- Small range of motion  

**Harder:**  
- Full rotation  
- Pause in each position  
- Slow tempo  

## Programming Options
- 2–4 sets × 6–10 reps per side  
- Include in warm-up or hip mobility routines  

## Benefits
- Improves hip internal/external rotation  
- Enhances hip control and flexibility  
- Supports squat, lunge, and rotational movements  

## Tips for Success
- Move controlled  
- Engage core  
- Breathe steadily
