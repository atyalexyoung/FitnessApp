## Form Cues
1. Sit in 90/90 position.  
2. Perform controlled external and internal rotation of the front leg.  
3. Maintain upright torso, core engaged.  

## Common Mistakes
- Leaning torso  
- Using momentum  
- Rounded lower back  

## Variations
**Easier:**  
- Smaller rotation  
- Hands support torso  

**Harder:**  
- Full rotation with slow tempo  
- Pause at end range  

## Programming Options
- 2–4 sets × 6–10 reps per side  
- Include in hip mobility circuits  

## Benefits
- Hip rotational mobility  
- Improves hip control for squats, lunges, and rotational sports  
- Enhances core stability  

## Tips for Success
- Move slowly and controlled  
- Engage core  
- Avoid twisting spine
