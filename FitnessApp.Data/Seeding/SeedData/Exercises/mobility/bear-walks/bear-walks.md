## Form Cues
1. Start on all fours, knees off the ground.  
2. Move opposite hand and foot forward, then switch.  
3. Keep core engaged, back flat.  

## Common Mistakes
- Hips sagging  
- Shoulder collapsing  
- Fast, uncontrolled movement  

## Variations
**Easier:**  
- Smaller steps  
- Rest hands on floor  

**Harder:**  
- Longer distances  
- Slow and controlled  
- Add lateral or diagonal movement  

## Programming Options
- 2–3 sets × 20–40s or 10–15 steps  
- Include in warm-up or mobility circuits  

## Benefits
- Shoulder and core stability  
- Hip mobility  
- Full-body coordination  

## Tips for Success
- Keep back flat  
- Engage core  
- Move slow and controlled
