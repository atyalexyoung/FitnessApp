## Form Cues
1. Clean kettlebell to shoulder rack.  
2. Dip slightly at knees, then drive upward to press kettlebell overhead.  
3. Lock out and stabilize core.  

## Common Mistakes
- Leaning back  
- Poor core engagement  
- Using arms only  

## Variations
**Easier:**  
- Use lighter kettlebell  
- Press slowly  

**Harder:**  
- Heavier kettlebell  
- Single-arm jerk  
- Explosive full-body movement  

## Programming Options
- 3–5 sets × 6–10 reps per arm  
- Include in full-body or shoulder circuits  

## Benefits
- Shoulder mobility and strength  
- Core stability  
- Explosive power development  

## Tips for Success
- Engage core  
- Drive with legs  
- Move controlled and fluid
