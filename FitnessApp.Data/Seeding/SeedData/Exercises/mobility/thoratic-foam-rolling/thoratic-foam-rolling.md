## Form Cues
1. Lie on foam roller along upper/mid back.  
2. Cross arms over chest or support head.  
3. Roll slowly from upper thoracic to mid-back, avoiding lower back.  

## Common Mistakes
- Rolling too fast  
- Rolling lower back  
- Arching back excessively  

## Variations
**Easier:**  
- Shorter roll distance  
- Use softer foam roller  

**Harder:**  
- Pause on tight spots  
- Use harder roller  
- Add arm movements  

## Programming Options
- 2–4 sets × 30–60s  
- Include pre-workout or recovery routines  

## Benefits
- Thoracic spine mobility  
- Reduces upper back tension  
- Improves posture and shoulder function  

## Tips for Success
- Roll slow and controlled  
- Breathe deeply  
- Focus on tight areas
