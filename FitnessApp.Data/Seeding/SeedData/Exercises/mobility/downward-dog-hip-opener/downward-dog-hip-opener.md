## Form Cues
1. Start in standard Downward Dog pose.  
2. Push hips back and slightly rotate leg externally.  
3. Keep spine long, core engaged, heels toward floor.  

## Common Mistakes
- Collapsing chest  
- Locking elbows  
- Overarching lower back  

## Variations
**Easier:**  
- Bend knees slightly  
- Reduce hip rotation  

**Harder:**  
- Straighten legs fully  
- Add leg lift or pulse  

## Programming Options
- 2–4 sets × 20–40s hold  
- Include as warm-up, mobility, or yoga flow  

## Benefits
- Hip and hamstring mobility  
- Shoulder stability  
- Improves posture  

## Tips for Success
- Move slowly into end range  
- Breathe deeply  
- Keep core engaged
