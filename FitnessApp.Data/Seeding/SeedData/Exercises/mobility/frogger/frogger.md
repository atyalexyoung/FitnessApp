## Form Cues
1. Start in deep squat, hands on floor.  
2. Jump or step feet forward, maintaining squat, then back.  
3. Keep torso upright and core engaged.  

## Common Mistakes
- Heels lifting  
- Leaning torso  
- Fast uncontrolled movement  

## Variations
**Easier:**  
- Smaller steps  
- Step instead of jump  

**Harder:**  
- Jump further  
- Add rotation  
- Slow tempo  

## Programming Options
- 2–3 sets × 10–15 reps  
- Include in warm-up or mobility flow  

## Benefits
- Hip, groin, and shoulder mobility  
- Full-body coordination  
- Core stability  

## Tips for Success
- Move controlled  
- Keep chest tall  
- Engage core
