## Form Cues
1. Sit on floor, knees bent, heels slightly raised.  
2. Lean back slightly, engage core.  
3. Rotate torso side to side, touching floor or holding weight.  

## Common Mistakes
- Rounding back  
- Using arms only  
- Fast uncontrolled twists  

## Variations
**Easier:**  
- Keep heels on floor  
- No weight  

**Harder:**  
- Hold medicine ball or dumbbell  
- Increase rotation speed  
- Extend legs straight  

## Programming Options
- 3–5 sets × 12–20 reps per side  
- Include in core circuits or HIIT  

## Benefits
- Oblique and rotational core strength  
- Core endurance  
- Improves stability and posture  

## Tips for Success
- Engage obliques, not just arms  
- Maintain slight lean back  
- Move controlled
