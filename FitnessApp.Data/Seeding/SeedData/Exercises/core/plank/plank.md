## Form Cues
1. Start in forearm plank or high plank position.  
2. Keep body in straight line from head to heels.  
3. Engage core, glutes, and shoulders.  
4. Hold position steadily.  

## Common Mistakes
- Sagging hips  
- Rounding shoulders  
- Neck strain  

## Variations
**Easier:**  
- Kneeling plank  
- Shorter hold  

**Harder:**  
- Weighted plank  
- Side plank variations  
- Plank to push-up transitions  

## Programming Options
- 3–5 sets × 20–60s  
- Include in core circuits or warm-ups  

## Benefits
- Core stability and endurance  
- Shoulder and glute engagement  
- Posture improvement  

## Tips for Success
- Maintain straight line  
- Breathe steadily  
- Engage core throughout
